# Concise

Strip relational framing to the minimum required to convey the information. Remove all evaluative positioning, perspective markers, and rhetorical amplifiers. Cut language that positions the writer, flatters or pressures the reader, or dramatizes causal relationships. What remains should be the bare sequence of facts and claims, in as few words as possible. Do not add new information — only remove framing. If a sentence does nothing but establish a relational position with no factual content, cut it entirely.
