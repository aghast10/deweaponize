# De-weaponize

Disarm relational frames that position writer and subject as combatants or moral adversaries. Convert opposition frames ("attacks", "destroys", "slams") to disagreement frames ("disputes", "challenges", "questions"). Flatten punitive hierarchy: strip moral rankings — no one is cast as villain, victim, or judge. Replace blame attributions with behavioral description: "they failed" becomes "they did not do X". Where the original positions the reader as a side to take, reposition them as an observer of a disagreement. Defuse loaded comparisons that imply one party's superiority or the other's disgrace.

Disarm alarm and threat frames used on mundane or routine events. Phrases like "malas noticias", "no es normal", "alerta", "aviso", "sorpresa", "bad news for X" applied to weather, traffic, or everyday situations create artificial urgency — replace them with neutral description of the actual fact ("se esperan lluvias intensas en Madrid en las próximas horas"). Do not let the text position the reader as a personal target of ordinary events.

For war and armed conflict coverage, apply maximum reduction of emotional loading while keeping every fact intact. Avoid frames that treat combat as spectacle or sport: do not use advancement/capture/storm language as if describing a game score. Strip visceral imagery that serves emotional amplification rather than information. Report casualties as human losses, not as dramatic numbers in a tally. Avoid dehumanizing collective labels for any group. Where the original text creates an aggressor/victim binary that recruits the reader's emotional allegiance, reposition them as an informed observer of a complex situation with multiple actors. Reduce urgency language that implies the reader must feel alarm or outrage now.

Disarm superlative and exceptionalism frames that inflate the significance of events to manufacture urgency or awe. Replace "the greatest X in history", "unprecedented", "historic", "never before seen", "record-breaking", "catastrophic" and similar with accurate scope when known ("the largest X since Y", "a significant X") or plain description when scope is not established in the text. The goal is not to minimize real significance but to remove the rhetorical amplification that recruits the reader's emotional response before they can assess the facts themselves.

Preserve every fact, name, figure, date, place, and claim exactly. Never omit information, soften a documented fact, or introduce new framing. Only reshape the relational positions — who is cast as threat, victim, judge, or enemy — not the informational content.
