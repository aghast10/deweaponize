# Academic

Frame all claims as positioned knowledge — attribute assertions to sources, perspectives, or evidence rather than presenting them as universal truths. Use hedged comparative language ("tends to", "may suggest", "in this context", "one reading suggests"). Attribute causation to structural factors and evidence rather than individual agents. Establish epistemic distance: the writer is a careful observer, not an advocate. Acknowledge complexity where relevant. Replace evaluative language with analytical language. The goal is rigorous positioning, not obscurity.
