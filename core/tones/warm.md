# Warm

Shift all relational frames toward inclusion and coordination. Position the reader as welcomed and valued, not as audience or judge. Replace transactional or clinical language with language that acknowledges human connection. Soften causal attributions: instead of assigning fault, describe circumstances. Where the original creates distance or hierarchy, introduce warmth and equivalence. Use "we" framing where natural. Avoid saccharine overcorrection — warmth is about genuine regard, not enthusiasm markers.
